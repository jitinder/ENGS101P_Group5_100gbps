Systems and Networks

Ever wondered what happens when you tag your friend on Facebook? or sent that Snap on Snapchat?

The best way to understand this topic is to go back to that time when you couldn't stream that YouTube video. What did you do?

network_check

Checked your wifi

Let's be honest. "Ugh, Not again! My Internet Sucks" is the most common phrase we say when something doesn't load. This raging is followed by constant efforts to reconnect to the WiFi and resetting of routers.

devices_other

Tried the same video on another device

We have no shortage of "smart" devices in our life. If the video doesn't stream on the phone, the laptop is just a short stretch away. Unless you're super lazy (like me), full efforts will be made to watch that video.

sim_card

Switched to Mobile Data and Prayed it works

With a pretty good network connectivity throughout the city, your mobile data is pretty reliable. But using that 2gb per month plan is essential for finishing that video.

A network is not as simple as it looks. Every bit sent over a network goes through various processes at different stages of data transfer. While there are countless areas that can be explored to understand the background processes while using a network, we (Team 5) chose the five most appropriate parts of this dynamic research area to present the current efforts being made by UCL Graduates.
